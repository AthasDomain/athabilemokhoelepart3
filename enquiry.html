FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions:

How does recycling reduce pollution?

Recycling reduces pollution by minimizing the amount of waste sent to landfills and incinerators, which in turn reduces the emission of harmful gases and pollutants into the air and water.

What are the benefits of planting trees?

Planting trees provides numerous benefits, including improving air quality, providing shade and cooling, supporting wildlife habitats, and enhancing the overall beauty of the environment.

What are some innovative waste management practices?

Some innovative waste management practices include composting organic waste, implementing circular economy principles like reusing materials, and using advanced technologies which include automated systems and sensors for waste sorting and processing.

What is composting?

Composting is the natural decomposition of organic waste, such as food scraps and yard trimmings, into a nutrient-rich soil mixture that can be used to improve soil health and plant growth.

What is hazardous waste?

Hazardous waste refers to materials that pose a risk to human health or the environment due to their toxic, corrosive, or reactive properties.
